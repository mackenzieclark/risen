## Genre

Horror-comedy / urban-fantasy parody. A send-up of gothic vampire fiction (*World of Darkness*, *Vampire: The Masquerade*) set in the present day, played for absurdist comedy rather than dread.

## Tone

Deadpan absurdist parody. The premise — cosmic, effortless vampiric power and the solemn reverence of an ancient society — is delivered with a completely straight face; the player character is the only one who finds any of it strange. The humour lives in three persistent gaps: god-tier power vs. petty out-of-touch banality; ancient gravitas vs. obvious silliness; and an unearned messianic destiny treated by everyone as self-evident. Voice should hold the line — never wink at the camera, never lampshade the joke directly.

## POV Approach

Second-person, present tense (Twine/Harlowe convention; reinforces the "this is happening to *you*, and you are baffled" register). _Confirm during outline seeding._

## Pacing

Brisk and scene-forward. Chapter One (the seduction) is intimate and slow-burn; the turn and society reveal accelerate into escalating absurdity. _Confirm during outline seeding._

## Target Length

Short interactive game to start — target a complete, playable Chapter One plus the turn (~10–18 passages across branches), expandable later. _Confirm during outline seeding._

## Style Notes

- **Stilted-exposition comedy:** elders, prophecies, and lore should be narrated in intentionally over-pompous, grandiose, slightly archaic phrasing — the *delivery* is the joke (reference: Brennan Lee Mulligan, *Never Stop Blowing Up*). Capital-Letter Nouns. Portentous pauses.
- **Mundane supernatural:** render world-bending power as casual and petty (reference: *What We Do in the Shadows*). Immortals bicker about etiquette and hosting duties.
- **Deadpan throughout:** straight-faced solemnity over absurd content. The PC's bewilderment is the audience surrogate.
- **The tailored pitch:** Chapter One's seduction must feel genuinely intimate and persuasive per archetype, not cartoonish — the satire is sharper if the manipulation lands.

## Preferences

- **Emphasize:** unearned "Chosen One" / self-insert power-fantasy as the satirical target; the trivial ease of world domination as a recurring joke; the gap between what vampires *could* do and what they actually waste eternity doing.
- **Archetypal openings (Chapter One backgrounds), each a wound + tailored offer:** Heartbroken Sad-Sack → eternal unconditional love; Bullied Outcast → power/adoration; Terminally Ill → never dying. (Candidates: Ambitious Failure, Burned-Out Caretaker, Forgotten Genius.)
- **Avoid:** genuine grimdark/edgelord tone; explaining the prophecy's mechanism (mystery preserves the joke); winking meta-humour that breaks the deadpan.
- **Title note:** "Risen" carries a double meaning — vampire rising from death + messianic "He Is Risen." Intentional.
