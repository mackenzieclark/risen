# Themes — Risen

The comedy is load-bearing: each theme is a setup, and the deadpan delivery is the punchline. None of these should ever be stated aloud by the text.

## 1. Wish Fulfillment Is the Trap
Every Chapter One pitch grants exactly what the player most wanted — and that is precisely the horror and the joke. Getting your deepest wish, perfectly, immediately, is how you get caught. The satire targets the fantasy itself.

## 2. Power Vs. Pettiness
Vampires are functionally gods from night one and use it to bicker about seating charts. The recurring gag is the chasm between what these beings *could* do (rule reality by Tuesday) and what they actually waste eternity on. World domination being trivially easy is a running joke, not a plot obstacle.

## 3. Unearned Destiny
The Chosen One is a nobody who got bitten on a Tuesday, and everyone believes in them completely, instantly, for no good reason. This is the central satirical target: self-insert/Mary-Sue power fantasy played with total sincerity by everyone except the bewildered player.

## 4. Tradition as Decay
The vampire society mistakes age for wisdom. It is an ancient institution that has calcified into a parody of itself — arcane etiquette, millennia-old grudges, profound seriousness about profoundly silly things. The Prophecy is the ultimate expression: a tradition repeated so long no one questions it.

## 5. Consent and Manipulation
You said yes. But the pitch was engineered to make refusal impossible. The thread of "did you actually choose any of this?" runs under the comedy — the only person treating the player's agency as real is the player.
