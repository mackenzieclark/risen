# Risen — Design Notes (WIP)

Scratchpad for the concept. Nothing here is final.

## Tone Bible (one line)

Cosmic power and ancient reverence, played completely deadpan, aimed at a bewildered nobody who never asked to be the messiah. The player is the only sane person in the room.

The three dials, always on:
1. **Effortless omnipotence** — every vampire is a god from night one.
2. **Out-of-touch banality** — the immortal society is petty, dated, and silly.
3. **Unearned destiny** — everyone instantly believes the player is The Risen One.

## Chapter One — Archetype Pitches

Each is a wound + the tailored offer that exploits it. The pitch is intimate, flattering, and manipulative. The player always accepts; the choice is *which wound*.

| Archetype | The Wound | The Tailored Offer |
|---|---|---|
| The Heartbroken Sad-Sack | Lonely, grieving, unloved | Eternal, unconditional, never-fading love |
| The Bullied Outcast | Invisible, powerless, humiliated | To be feared, adored, unignorable |
| The Terminally Ill | Out of time | Simply never dying |
| The Ambitious Failure *(maybe)* | Talented, overlooked, broke | Limitless time and power to finally "make it" |
| The Burned-Out Caretaker *(maybe)* | Drained, taken for granted | Strength without end; never being the weak one again |
| The Forgotten Genius *(maybe)* | Brilliant, ignored, irrelevant | Centuries to be proven right |

Open: how many ship at launch; how divergent each playthrough is; whether the chosen wound recurs as a thematic thread (e.g. the love-promised player keeps being offered hollow versions of love).

## The Turn

Beat order to prototype:
1. Acceptance / the bite (end of Chapter One).
2. Waking up changed — discover the absurd power set in a low-stakes, comedic way (mind-controlling a barista by accident, etc.).
3. The realization: *I could just... take over the world. Easily. Why hasn't anyone?*
4. First contact with the society — reverence is instant and unexplained.

## The Society

- Impossibly ancient, rigidly hierarchical, parody-of-itself.
- Obsessed with etiquette, precedence, and millennia-old grudges.
- Profoundly serious about profoundly silly things.
- Could rewrite reality; argues about seating and hosting duties instead.
- Elders narrate in stilted, over-pompous grandeur (the BLM register).

## The Prophecy ("Risen")

- Long tradition of a foretold vampire messiah who unites the clans and rules the world "as was Written."
- Everyone instantly, unshakeably believes it's the player. The "evidence" is flimsy and constantly re-cited.
- Keep the *mechanism* mysterious — explaining it kills the joke. The comedy is in unearned, unquestioned certainty.
- Title double meaning: vampire rising from death + messianic "He Is Risen."

## Open Questions

- Mechanism of selection — how to keep it bewildering, not explained.
- Player agency over the Chosen One role: embrace / resist / exploit / flee?
- Is "world domination is trivially easy" an actual mechanic or a sustained gag?
- Engine: Harlowe (simpler) vs SugarCube (more state/macros) — depends on how stat-driven it gets.

## Influences (reference)

- *What We Do in the Shadows* — mundane supernatural pettiness.
- *World of Darkness / Vampire: The Masquerade* — the thing being parodied.
- Brennan Lee Mulligan, *Never Stop Blowing Up* — stilted, grandiose exposition as comedy.
- Chosen One / self-insert power-fantasy fanfiction — the satirized form.
